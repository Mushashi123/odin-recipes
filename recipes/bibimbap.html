Bibimbap (Korean Rice With Mixed Vegetables)

Description

Bibimbap is a delicious and colorful Korean dish that has gained international popularity for its harmonious blend of flavors and textures. The name "bibimbap" translates to "mixed rice" in English, which perfectly describes the essence of this dish. At its core, bibimbap consists of a bed of steamed rice topped with a vibrant array of sautéed and seasoned vegetables, often including ingredients like spinach, bean sprouts , carrots, and mushrooms. What sets bibimbap apart is the addition of a protein source, which can range from thinly sliced marinated beef (known as bulgogi) to tofu or even a fried egg. To bring it all together, a spicy and savory gochujang sauce is typically drizzled over the top, adding a punch of flavor that ties the elements of the dish together. The final touch is a sprinkling of sesame seeds and sometimes crispy, toasted seaweed, creating a visually stunning and incredibly satisfying meal.

Bibimbap is not only celebrated for its delightful taste but also its presentation. Served in a hot stone bowl called a "dolsot," the rice on the bottom becomes beautifully crisp as it comes into contact with the heated surface. This contrast in textures, from the crunchy rice to the tender vegetables and flavorful meat or tofu, adds to the overall appeal of the dish. The customizable nature of bibimbap makes it a favorite among food enthusiasts, as diners can tailor the ingredients to suit their preferences. Whether enjoyed at a Korean restaurant or prepared at home, bibimbap is a flavorful, nutritious, and visually striking dish that embodies the essence of Korean cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 English cucumber, cut into matchsticks
  • ¼ cup gochujang (Korean hot pepper paste) (Optional)
  • 1 bunch fresh spinach, cut into thin strips
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons olive oil, divided
  • 2 carrots, cut into matchsticks
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 pinch red pepper flakes
  • 1 pound thinly-sliced beef top round steak
  • 4 large eggs
  • 4 cups cooked white rice
  • 4 teaspoons toasted sesame oil, divided
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • 2 teaspoons gochujang (Korean hot pepper paste), divided (Optional)

Steps

  1. Step 1

    Stir together cucumber pieces and gochujang paste in a bowl; set aside.

  2. Step 2

    Bring about 2 cups water to a boil in a large nonstick skillet and stir in spinach; cook until bright green and wilted, 2 to 3 minutes.

  3. Step 3

    Drain spinach and squeeze out as much moisture as possible; set spinach aside in a bowl and stir in soy sauce.

  4. Step 4

    Heat 1 teaspoon olive oil in a large nonstick skillet; cook and stir carrots until softened, about 3 minutes.

  5. Step 5

    Stir in garlic and cook just until fragrant, about 1 minute. Stir in cucumber mixture; sprinkle with red pepper flakes. Set carrot mixture aside in a bowl.

  6. Step 6

    Brown beef in a clean nonstick skillet over medium heat, about 5 minutes per side; set aside.

  7. Step 7

    Heat remaining 1 teaspoon olive oil in another nonstick skillet over medium-low heat. Fry eggs just on one side until yolks are runny, but whites are firm, 2 to 4 minutes.

  8. Step 8

    Divide cooked rice into 4 large serving bowls; top with spinach mixture, a few pieces of beef, and cucumber mixture. Place 1 egg atop each serving. Drizzle each bowl with 1 teaspoon sesame oil, a sprinkle of sesame seeds, and a small amount of gochujang paste if desired.
